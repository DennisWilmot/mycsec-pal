## Question 1

**Purpose/setting answer:** To inform readers about how a local community garden provides food, practical skills and social benefits to neighbourhood residents.

**Accepted summary points:**
- Produces food that supplements household meals and distributes surplus to families in need.
- Provides practical learning opportunities for children and young people (gardening, construction, composting).
- Strengthens community ties through shared activities, meetings and cultural exchange (potlucks, mutual support).
- Relies on community funding and volunteer organisation, showing local resilience and potential for replication.

**Sample summary (39 words):** The community garden supplies vegetables to families and shares surplus with those in need, offers hands-on learning for children and young people (gardening and construction), and strengthens social ties through shared work, cultural exchange and community-led funding and repairs.

## Question 3

**Purpose/setting answer:** A night ferry crossing with sea and engine sounds, dim island lights behind, cool salty air on deck and an intimate, quiet atmosphere among diverse passengers.

**Accepted summary points:**
- She stands on deck feeling the chill spray and contemplates a farewell letter and leaving the island.

## Question 6

**Purpose/setting answer:** To persuade Caribbean secondary-school stakeholders to adopt later school start times to improve students’ sleep, safety, mental health and academic performance.

**Accepted summary points:**
- Later school start times (e.g., 8:30 a.m.) help teenagers get more sleep, which improves concentration, memory and test performance.
- Starting school later reduces early-morning travel in darkness and lowers the risk of accidents involving students.
- Adequate sleep from later starts leads to better mental health, reducing irritability, anxiety and depression among adolescents.
- Scheduling concerns about extracurriculars and jobs can be managed by adjusting activity times and coordinating with community partners.
- Stakeholders should form committees and run pilot trials to evaluate and implement later start times across districts.

**Sample summary (33 words):** The writer urges Caribbean schools to start later so teens get more sleep, boosting learning, safety and mental health; scheduling issues can be managed, and stakeholders should run pilots to adopt the change.
